Background & purpose

The Brand Insights & Analytics Global Stat Pack offers reliable, representative insights cleared for external sharing. Use these to build data-driven comms pitches or help Marketing understand TikTok user attitudes — with concrete numbers rather than generalizations.

For example: instead of saying "TikTok users are passionate about beauty," you can say "X% of TikTok users are interested in beauty — and they're Nx more likely to say so than non-users."

All insights are pulled from GWI (GlobalWebIndex) and updated on a yearly basis.

What is GWI?

GWI (GlobalWebIndex) is an audience research company that fields surveys quarterly across 50+ countries to understand consumer attitudes and behaviors. It also collects self-reported demographic data including age, gender, and household composition.

Scale & reach
Surveys internet users across 50+ countries in their local language
Consistent fielding
Most questions asked quarterly over several years — enabling trend analysis
Representative samples
Uses quotas and weights to ensure representative online samples
Breadth of topics
Covers attitudes, interests, sports, lifestyle, social media, devices, and more
How to use these data
  • ✓ OK to share
    You can share individual metrics publicly with press and in external-facing materials.
  • ✗ Do not share
    Do not share docs in full externally — not even just your country's section. This is an internal reference for when you need a specific metric or package of metrics.
  • Always review data before sharing to ensure it supports a positive external narrative and won't present brand risk. Examples of what to avoid:
    ❌ Sharing "60% of TikTok users are interested in healthy eating" if facing SkinnyTok allegations
    ❌ Sharing "23% of TikTok users like reading" if it sounds like users aren't literary/educated
  • Reframing is fine. Turn a percentage into a more compelling talking point:
    "16% of TikTok users" → "1 in 6 TikTok users"
    "32% of TikTok users" → "one third of TikTok users"
  • Review markets individually. Cultural variations affect how positively or negatively people respond — cross-market comparisons can be misleading.
  • Data shared externally must be 18+. Although GWI offers 16+ data, external use requires 18+. Consult Brand Insights & Analytics if <18 data is needed for internal planning.
  • Cite the data: All data comes from GWI, a third-party research database (subscription tool — not TikTok proprietary data). Results are based on Q1–Q4 2025 reports.
    Sample citation: GWI, Q1–Q4 2025. [Market] respondents, age 18+. TikTok users are defined based on self-reported usage of monthly or greater frequency.
